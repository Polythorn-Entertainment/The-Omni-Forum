Privacy

How OmniForum Handles Data

This forum stores only the information needed to run accounts, discussions, and moderation workflows. If this policy changes materially, the updated version should replace this page.

What we collect

OmniForum stores account details you submit directly, including username, password hash, profile bio, avatar, forum posts, thread activity, likes, bookmarks, thread follows, direct messages, notifications, user reports, and contact-form messages. If you include a Discord username in the contact form, that handle is stored with the notice for staff review. Passwords are stored as hashes, not plain text.

Session and login data

When you log in, the site stores a session cookie so your browser stays signed in. Session records, recent activity timestamps, IP metadata, and browser user-agent strings are used to support authentication, “online now” indicators, security review, and session management.

Why we use the data

Your data is used to run the forum, display your contributions, honor your DM and notification preferences, deliver direct messages and alerts, manage roles, support moderation and reports, and respond to support or policy issues submitted through the contact form.

Who can see what

Public forum content is visible to users with access to the relevant section. Direct messages are private to the participants unless disclosure is required for safety, abuse handling, or legal compliance. Moderators and admins may review posts, account metadata relevant to moderation, session metadata relevant to abuse or recovery review, reports submitted by users, and contact submissions sent to staff.

Contact messages

Messages sent through the contact form are stored in the forum database and surfaced to moderators/admins through a staff inbox so they can review and respond. Email support is not part of this forum workflow; if you want staff to have an off-site handle to reference, include your Discord username in the form.

Data retention

Content and account data remain stored until removed by staff, deleted through moderation, or intentionally cleared from the underlying database by site operators.

Security limits

OmniForum uses reasonable technical protections for a small forum application, but no internet service can promise perfect security. Do not share secrets or sensitive personal data you would not want exposed.
